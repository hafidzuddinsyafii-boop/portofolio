Hello Everyone!

Who am i?

Hi! I'm Naufal Syafii Hafidzuddin, but you can call me Syafii. I was born in Sumedang on March 17, 2009, though I grew up navigating the busy streets of East Jakarta. I'm a Computer Science student who is honestly just obsessed with how things work behind the screen. Being one of five siblings—I have two brothers and two sisters—means my house was always loud and full of movement, which surprisingly taught me a lot about patience and how to stand my ground in a crowded environment. Growing up in the eastern part of Jakarta also gave me a front-row seat to how fast technology changes a city, and that’s where my curiosity for the digital world actually started. I might be younger than most people in this industry, but I don't see my age or my "newbie" status as a barrier; I see it as a head start to learn as much as possible before I hit the professional world.

Moving from the comfort of my family home in Jakarta to a boarding school in Bogor was a big step for me, but it was necessary to chase what I’m passionate about. Right now, I’m fully immersed in my studies at SMK TI Bazma, where I spend most of my days breaking and fixing things in the world of web development and networking. I didn't want to just be a consumer of technology; I wanted to be the one building the tools people use every day. Living in the dorms at Bazma has forced me to become more disciplined and independent, traits that I think are just as important for a developer as knowing how to write clean code. I'm focusing my energy on mastering modern web frameworks and refining my creative instincts through graphic design, blending tactical aesthetics with functional layouts.

I believe that the best way to learn is by doing, which is why I’m always looking for new challenges to tackle. Whether it’s debugging a stubborn piece of CSS or figuring out how to optimize a deployment on Netlify, I enjoy the process of turning a problem into a solution. My journey from Sumedang to Jakarta, and now to the hills of Bogor, has given me a pretty unique perspective on how technology can bridge gaps between different communities. I’m not just here to follow a curriculum; I’m here to build a solid foundation so that I can eventually contribute something meaningful to the global tech ecosystem. I’m excited about what’s next, and I’m ready to keep pushing my limits, one project at a time, while keeping that same curiosity I had when I first started this journey.

Educational Background

My academic journey has been a series of adaptations that have shaped my mental resilience today. Before landing at SMK TI Bazma, I spent my junior high school years at Pondok Pesantren Al-Hassan in Bekasi. Within the pesantren, I realized a significant gap: while my spiritual discipline was honed, I felt far behind in my technological mastery compared to the rapidly evolving outside world. This awareness of the 'digital gap' was the main catalyst for me to take a bold step and pursue a specialized vocational education at SMK TI Bazma. Ultimately, I found the right platform to catch up and transform my curiosity into measurable expertise.

Basic Education Track Record

My adaptability has been tested since elementary school, when I transferred schools three times. My journey began at SDIT Rabbaniyyun, before finally transferring to Pesantren Tahfidz Askar Kauny in second grade to further my Quran memorization. After two intensive years there, I completed my primary education at SDI Nailufar. This series of moves between Jakarta and Bekasi was more than just a change of location; it was a character-building process that made me fearless in the face of drastic environmental changes, a crucial trait in the ever-changing technology industry.
